At A Glance

Why edge profiles matter

The edge is where your countertop meets daily life. It influences safety, maintenance, and the overall aesthetic.

Style & Mood

Softened edges feel approachable and classic, while crisp bevels and mitered details read bold and architectural.

Comfort & Safety

Rounded profiles reduce sharp corners and are great for families, busy kitchens, and high-traffic workspaces.

Maintenance

Simpler profiles wipe clean quickly. Ornate or layered edges add drama but can need more detailed cleaning.

Selection Guide

How to choose the right edge

Balance material thickness, design style, and how you plan to use the space.

Think about the room

Modern kitchens often pair well with chamfered or mitered edges, while traditional spaces shine with ogee or bullnose profiles. Consider the cabinets, lighting, and hardware to keep the look cohesive.

  • Choose eased or beveled edges for clean, minimalist spaces.
  • Use bullnose styles for a soft, family-friendly feel.
  • Elevate luxury kitchens with ornate ogee profiles.
  • Go mitered when you want a thicker, seamless slab look.

Fayetteville FAQs

Answers to the questions we hear before template day.

FAQ 1

When should we schedule the final measure?

Schedule your final measure once cabinets are set and any site conditions that affect fit are complete. That keeps fabrication accurate and prevents avoidable delays.

FAQ 2

Do I need to be there for installation?

We recommend having the decision-maker onsite for the final measure and available on installation day so sink placement, seam locations, and finishing details can be confirmed quickly.

FAQ 3

Can I compare materials, edges, or backsplash options first?

Yes. We help clients compare slabs, surface types, edge profiles, and backsplash pairings before fabrication so the final install feels intentional, not rushed.
