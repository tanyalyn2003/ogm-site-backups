Care & Maintenance

Beautiful countertops deserve proper care. Our care and maintenance guide covers daily cleaning, long-term sealing recommendations, stain removal tips, and the dos and do nots for each material type. With the right routine, your stone will look flawless for years to come.

Learn daily cleaning, sealing schedules, stain removal, and material-specific care tips.

General Countertop Care Tips

Regardless of the material, following these general care guidelines will help maintain your countertops for years to come:

  • Clean Regularly: Use a soft cloth or sponge with mild dish soap and warm water to clean spills and everyday grime.
  • Avoid Abrasives: Steer clear of abrasive pads or cleaners that could scratch or dull the surface.
  • Wipe Spills Quickly: Especially for porous materials like marble and granite, clean up spills immediately to prevent staining.
  • Use Cutting Boards: Always use a cutting board to avoid scratching the surface, even on scratch-resistant materials.
  • Protect from Heat: Use trivets or hot pads under hot pots and pans to prevent heat damage.

Maintenance Schedule

Task Frequency Notes
Daily Cleaning Daily Use soft cloth, water, and mild soap.
Stain Spot Treatment As Needed Use material-specific cleaners.
Sealing (Granite, Marble, Quartzite) Annually or Bi-Annually Check with a water bead test to assess the seal.
Deep Cleaning Monthly Use non-abrasive or material-safe cleaners.

Quick Reference: Countertop Care Dos and Do Nots

Material Requires Sealing Heat Resistant Avoid Recommended Cleaner
Granite Yes (1–2 years) Yes Acidic cleaners, oil-based products Mild soap, water
Quartz No Moderate Abrasives, scouring pads, high heat Gentle soap, water
Quartzite Yes (Annually) Yes Acidic or abrasive cleaners pH-neutral cleaner
Marble Yes (6–12 months) No Acidic substances, harsh chemicals pH-neutral cleaner

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using Harsh Chemicals: Acidic or alkaline cleaners can cause etching or discoloration. Stick to pH-neutral solutions.
  • Skipping Sealing: For porous materials like granite and marble, not sealing can lead to stains and water damage.
  • Placing Hot Pans Directly on the Surface: Even on heat-resistant materials like granite, sudden temperature changes can cause cracks.
  • Ignoring Spills: Substances like wine, coffee, and oil can penetrate porous surfaces quickly, leading to permanent stains.

Tips for Long-Term Care

  • Use Coasters: Protect the surface from water rings and acidic beverages.
  • Inspect Regularly: Look for signs of wear, stains, or dullness, and address issues promptly.
  • Polish Occasionally: For materials like granite and marble, polishing can restore shine and beauty.

By following these care tips, you will extend the life of your countertops and keep them looking as stunning as the day they were installed. If you need professional sealing or have questions about maintaining your specific material, contact Olive Glass & Marble for expert advice!

The Process

Installation

Our installation process is designed for precision and peace of mind. Learn what to expect on installation day, how to prepare your home, and how our team ensures a perfect fit every time. From final measurements to quality checks, we walk you through each step so there are no surprises — just a stunning finished result.

Step-by-step guide to preparation, installation day, and post-installation care.

Before Installation Day — How to Prepare Your Space

Clear the Work Area

  • Remove items from countertops, shelves, and surrounding surfaces.
  • Clear a pathway for installers to bring slabs inside safely.
  • For commercial sites, ensure access to loading areas and elevator clearances.

Disconnect Appliances (If Needed)

  • Sinks, faucets, cooktops, and gas/electric connections should be uninstalled before the team arrives unless OGM has arranged this service.
  • Undermount sink templates must be approved prior to fabrication.

Cabinets Must Be Level & Secure

  • Cabinets must be permanently installed, anchored, and level within industry tolerance.
  • Any adjustments must be completed before installation — countertops cannot be installed on cabinets that are out of level or unstable.

Plumbing & Electrical Planning

  • Schedule plumbers/electricians for after installation.
  • Do not reconnect water or gas the same day unless advised.

The Day of Installation — Step-by-Step Process

Arrival & Site Walkthrough

The OGM team arrives within your scheduled window. We walk through the project with you, confirm layout, seams, edge profiles, sink placement, backsplash plans, and any special details.

Protection of Your Home or Workspace

Floors, doorways, and high-traffic areas are protected as needed. For commercial sites, we follow all safety protocols and PPE requirements.

Bringing in the Slabs

Our crew carefully transports the stone. Slabs are moved using dollies, clamps, and safety equipment to protect both your space and the stone.

Dry-Fit & Alignment

  • Each piece is placed to confirm fit.
  • Seams, corners, and cutouts are checked to ensure proper alignment.
  • Any micro-adjustments are completed on-site with precise tools.

Sink & Cooktop Setting

  • Undermount sinks are secured with adhesives and mounting brackets.
  • Farmhouse/apron sinks are shimmed and supported before stone is placed.
  • Cooktop openings are confirmed for proper clearance.

Securing the Countertops

  • Stone is set with high-strength adhesives.
  • Seams are joined, leveled, and color-matched for minimal visibility.
  • Countertops are checked for stability and proper overhang.

Final Quality Check

Before our team leaves, we perform a full inspection:

  • Confirm seams are tight and level
  • Ensure sink reveals match the approved template
  • Check edges for smoothness and consistency
  • Verify overhangs, supports, and cabinet alignment
  • Wipe down surfaces and remove any installation dust
  • Review care & maintenance basics with you
  • Answer all questions before closing out the job

For commercial projects, we also provide:

  • Punch-list completion
  • Sign-off documentation
  • Coordination with general contractors or project managers

After Installation — Important Guidelines

Plumbing & Electrical

  • Schedule reconnection 24 hours after installation unless otherwise advised.
  • Avoid putting stress on the sink or cooktop cutouts immediately.

Avoid Heavy Weight on Overhangs

Do not lean on unsupported bar tops or large overhangs.

Initial Sealing (If Applicable)

Natural stones like granite or marble may be sealed at installation. Reseal intervals depend on the material and usage.

Adhesive Cure Time

Adhesives need proper time to cure; avoid moving heavy appliances back into place for at least 24 hours.

When to Contact Us

Reach out to OGM if you notice:

  • Any shifting or settling within the first 48 hours
  • Questions about maintenance or sealing
  • Issues with plumbing fitment or sink installation
  • Concerns about seams, leveling, or finish

Materials & Product Selection

What is the difference between granite, marble, quartz, and quartzite?

Granite and quartzite are natural stones that offer excellent durability and heat resistance. Marble provides a softer, classic look but requires more maintenance. Quartz is man-made, low-maintenance, and consistent in pattern but not recommended for outdoor use due to UV sensitivity. View detailed comparison →

Can I use quartz outdoors?

No. Quartz contains resins that can discolor, fade, or warp in sunlight. Granite and quartzite are better choices for outdoor kitchens.

Are granite and quartzite heat-proof?

Yes, they handle high heat extremely well — though trivets are still recommended. Quartz is not heat-proof and can scorch.

Do you carry full slabs for viewing?

Yes. You can visit our slab yard to hand-select your stone before fabrication. View our service areas →

Timelines & Process

How long does the full process take?

Most projects take 7–14 business days from template/measure to installation, depending on material availability and project complexity.

Do I need to be home during installation?

Yes. A decision-maker should be present to approve final placement, seam locations, and sink fitment. Learn about installation day →

What needs to be done before measurement?

Cabinets must be permanently installed, level, and secured. Sinks, faucets, and appliances that affect measurement should be on-site.

Sinks, Faucets & Cooktops

Can you install my sink?

We secure undermount sinks during installation. Plumbing reconnection must be handled by your plumber after the stone has cured.

Can I keep my existing sink?

Yes — as long as it is compatible with the material and cutout size.

What is the difference between an undermount and a drop-in sink?

Undermounts mount below the stone for easy cleaning and a streamlined look. Drop-in sinks sit above the surface and require visible rims.

Care & Maintenance

Do natural stone countertops need to be sealed?

Granite and marble generally require sealing. Quartzite depends on the specific stone. Quartz does not require sealing. View care guide →

How often should I reseal my countertops?

Most stones benefit from annual sealing, but your stone's porosity may vary. We can recommend a schedule based on your slab.

What cleaners are safe to use?

Use mild soap or stone-safe cleaners. Avoid bleach, vinegar, abrasive pads, or harsh chemicals.
